A garage that exposes the physics, not hides it.

The setup screen is where serious sim racers live. This proposal models the car the way Assetto Corsa, rFactor 2, Automobilista 2, and Rennsport do — multi-point tire contact, Pacejka-style slip curves, real spring and damper rates, surface-aware grip, and aero that reacts to ride height. Visuals stay friendly to Kenney's low-poly racing kit, but the language and controls are motorsport.

Why a 5-point probe

Single-point raycasts mis-handle curbs and stepped surfaces. Probing at the front, rear, center, and both edges of each contact patch matches what ACC adopted in 1.0.7 and is cheap with Jolt's shape casting.

Visuals stay friendly

The cars are Kenney low-poly, but the language is motorsport. Setup uses real units (psi, mm, N/mm). Players can opt into assists and assists run on top of physics, never as faked input.

Telemetry first

Debug telemetry ships in V1, not as a stretch. Vertical load, slip, aligning torque, and surface ID are needed to validate the tire and suspension model before any car feels right.
